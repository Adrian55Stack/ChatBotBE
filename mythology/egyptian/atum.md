# Atum
Culture: Egyptian

Atum is one of the oldest Egyptian deities, the creator god who emerged from the primordial waters of Nun.
He is self-created, having brought himself into existence through sheer will and creative force.
Atum created the first divine pair, Shu and Tefnut, from himself, setting the process of creation in motion.
He is associated with the setting sun and the completed cycle of the day, representing the sun at dusk.
Atum is depicted as a man wearing the double crown of Upper and Lower Egypt, or sometimes as a serpent.
At the end of time, Atum is said to dissolve back into the primordial waters of Nun, ending all creation.
