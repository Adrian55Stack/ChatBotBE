# Tefnut
Culture: Egyptian

Tefnut is the Egyptian goddess of moisture, rain, dew, and the moisture-laden air.
She is the daughter of Atum and the twin sister of Shu, god of air and light.
Together with Shu, Tefnut forms a complementary pair representing the atmospheric conditions necessary for life.
She is depicted as a lioness-headed woman, symbolizing the fierce and untameable nature of natural forces.
In one myth, Tefnut became enraged and fled to Nubia as a lioness, causing drought and desolation in Egypt.
The gods sent Thoth to persuade her to return, and her homecoming was celebrated as the return of fertility and abundance.
