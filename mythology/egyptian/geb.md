# Geb
Culture: Egyptian

Geb is the Egyptian god of the earth and the physical embodiment of the ground upon which all life exists.
He is the son of Shu and Tefnut, and the husband of Nut, the sky goddess.
Geb and Nut were famously separated by their father Shu, who holds Nut above Geb for eternity.
He is the father of Osiris, Isis, Seth, and Nephthys, the principal gods of the Osirian myth cycle.
Geb is depicted as a man lying on the ground, often with green skin to represent vegetation and the fertility of the earth.
His laughter is said to cause earthquakes, and his body is the land itself, from which all plants and creatures spring.
