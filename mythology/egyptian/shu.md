# Shu
Culture: Egyptian

Shu is the Egyptian god of air, light, and the space between earth and sky.
He is the son of Atum and the twin brother of Tefnut, goddess of moisture.
Shu's primary role is to hold up the sky goddess Nut, separating her from the earth god Geb.
He is depicted as a man wearing an ostrich feather on his head, standing with his arms raised to support the heavens.
Shu personifies the dry, warm air and the sunlight that fills the space between earth and sky.
Together with Tefnut, Shu is the parent of Geb and Nut, making him a foundational figure in Egyptian divine genealogy.
