# Nut
Culture: Egyptian

Nut is the Egyptian goddess of the sky, stars, and the heavens, arching over the earth in an eternal embrace.
She is the daughter of Shu and Tefnut, and the wife of Geb, the earth god.
Nut is depicted as a woman whose elongated body forms the vault of the sky, covered in stars.
She swallows the sun each evening and gives birth to it again each morning, sustaining the eternal cycle of day and night.
Nut is the mother of Osiris, Isis, Seth, and Nephthys, and plays a protective role over the dead.
She is often painted on the inside of coffin lids, so that the deceased rests within her protective embrace.
