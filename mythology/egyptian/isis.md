# Isis
Culture: Egyptian

Isis is one of the most important goddesses of ancient Egypt, associated with magic, healing, motherhood, and rebirth.
She is the daughter of Geb and Nut, sister and wife of Osiris, and mother of Horus.
When Osiris was killed and dismembered by Set, Isis gathered his scattered body parts and used her magic to resurrect him.
She conceived Horus with the resurrected Osiris and raised him in secret in the marshes of the Nile Delta.
Isis is depicted wearing a throne-shaped headdress and is often shown nursing the infant Horus.
Her cult spread beyond Egypt throughout the Greco-Roman world, making her one of the most widely worshipped ancient deities.
