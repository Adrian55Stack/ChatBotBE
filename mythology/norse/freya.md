# Freya
Culture: Norse

Freya is the Norse goddess of love, fertility, beauty, war, and death.
She belongs to the Vanir tribe of gods and came to Asgard as part of a peace agreement between the Aesir and Vanir.
Freya possesses a cloak of falcon feathers that grants the power of flight, and a necklace called Brísingamen.
She rides a chariot pulled by two large cats and is accompanied by her boar Hildisvíni.
Freya claims half of those slain in battle, bringing them to her hall Fólkvangr, while Odin takes the other half to Valhalla.
She is also associated with the practice of seiðr, a form of Norse magic and divination.
