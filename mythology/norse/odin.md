# Odin
Culture: Norse

Odin is the Allfather of the Norse pantheon, god of wisdom, war, death, and poetry.
He sacrificed one of his eyes at Mimir's Well in exchange for cosmic wisdom.
Odin hung himself on Yggdrasil, the World Tree, for nine days to discover the runes.
He rides the eight-legged horse Sleipnir and is accompanied by two ravens, Huginn and Muninn.
Odin rules Asgard and presides over Valhalla, where slain warriors feast until Ragnarök.
During Ragnarök, Odin is swallowed by the great wolf Fenrir.
