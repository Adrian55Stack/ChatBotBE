# Baldr
Culture: Norse

Baldr is the Norse god of light, purity, beauty, and righteousness, beloved by all beings.
He is the son of Odin and Frigg, and his death is one of the most sorrowful events in Norse mythology.
Frigg made every object in existence swear not to harm Baldr, except the mistletoe, which she deemed too young.
Loki discovered this weakness and tricked the blind god Höðr into throwing mistletoe at Baldr, killing him instantly.
Baldr descended to Hel's realm, where he remains until after Ragnarök.
After Ragnarök, Baldr returns from the dead to help rule a renewed and reborn world.
