# Loki
Culture: Norse

Loki is the Norse trickster god, a shape-shifter of cunning intellect and unpredictable nature.
He is the son of the giants Fárbauti and Laufey, yet counts among the Aesir gods of Asgard.
Loki is the father of Fenrir the wolf, Jörmungandr the serpent, and Hel, ruler of the dead.
He orchestrated the death of Baldr by tricking the blind god Höðr into throwing mistletoe at him.
As punishment, Loki was bound beneath a mountain with serpent venom dripping onto his face.
At Ragnarök, Loki breaks free and leads the forces of chaos against the gods of Asgard.
