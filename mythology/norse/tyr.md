# Tyr
Culture: Norse

Tyr is the Norse god of law, justice, and heroic glory, one of the oldest of the Aesir gods.
He is associated with the formal procedures of war, legal contracts, and righteous combat.
Tyr sacrificed his right hand so that the other gods could bind the great wolf Fenrir with the magical fetter Gleipnir.
Fenrir agreed to be bound only if one of the gods placed their hand in his mouth as a pledge of good faith.
When Fenrir discovered he could not break free, he bit off Tyr's hand at the wrist.
During Ragnarök, Tyr slays the hound Garm but dies from the wounds inflicted in the battle.
