# Hephaestus
Culture: Greek

Hephaestus is the Greek god of fire, metalworking, craftsmanship, and the forge.
He is the son of Zeus and Hera, though some accounts say Hera bore him alone out of spite.
Hephaestus is the only Olympian god depicted as physically imperfect, having been lamed after being thrown from Olympus.
Despite his ungainly appearance, he is the finest craftsman among the gods, forging divine weapons and wondrous artifacts.
His creations include Achilles's armor, Hermes's winged helmet, Eros's golden arrows, and the chains that bound Prometheus.
Hephaestus was married to Aphrodite, the goddess of love, though their marriage was notoriously troubled.
