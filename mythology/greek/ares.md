# Ares
Culture: Greek

Ares is the Greek god of war, violence, and the chaos of battle.
He is the son of Zeus and Hera, and one of the twelve Olympians, though widely feared and disliked by the other gods.
Unlike Athena, who represents strategic warfare, Ares embodies the brutal and destructive aspects of conflict.
He had a long love affair with Aphrodite, goddess of love, much to the humiliation of her husband Hephaestus.
Ares was wounded in battle by the mortal Diomedes, who was aided by Athena, and fled to Olympus in shame.
Despite his fierce nature, Ares was not always victorious, often suffering defeats that reflected Greek ambivalence toward war.
