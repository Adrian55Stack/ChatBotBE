# Poseidon
Culture: Greek

Poseidon is the Greek god of the sea, earthquakes, storms, and horses.
He is the brother of Zeus and Hades, and the three divided dominion over the world after overthrowing the Titans.
Poseidon wields a trident, which he uses to stir the seas, shatter rock, and trigger earthquakes.
He competed with Athena for patronage of Athens, offering the city a saltwater spring, but lost to her gift of the olive tree.
Poseidon is the father of the Cyclops Polyphemus and bore a long grudge against Odysseus for blinding his son.
He dwells in an underwater palace and commands the creatures of the deep, including horses born from sea foam.
