# Hermes
Culture: Greek

Hermes is the Greek god of travel, commerce, communication, thieves, trickery, and the messenger of the gods.
He is the son of Zeus and the Pleiad Maia, and one of the most cunning and versatile of the Olympians.
On the day of his birth, Hermes stole Apollo's cattle and invented the lyre from a tortoise shell to appease him.
He wears winged sandals and a winged helmet, allowing him to move swiftly between the mortal and divine realms.
Hermes serves as the psychopomp, guiding the souls of the dead to the underworld of Hades.
He is the patron of merchants, travelers, and thieves alike, presiding over all forms of exchange and communication.
